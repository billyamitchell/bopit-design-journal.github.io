30 minute toy designs from childhood
I made a simple joint for two objects that was inspired by the tactile aspects of playing with legos I had as a kid. I wanted to replicate the *snap* that I found satisfying when I was younger and still do today. I used a plastic connection for the joint in order to provide the right feel to the point of connection. I used a soft mdf wood that was rounded off on one side to provide something that was nice to hold onto and manipulate the joint with your hands.

ROBIONS GAMEPLAY:
Who is it for?

  • Young Adult (Age 15-18) -- or younger?
  • Interested in strategy games
What is it?
  • A turn based strategy game that takes place between 4 players/robots (1-4 people + AI) on a modular tile board.
  • The goal is to dominate the board and convert every robot to your side
    • There is a environmental/ecological component to the world that is illustrated on the tiles, woven into the story/goal, and visualized through the encounters.
    • The tiles have 2 groups: ecological and industrial
    • A “side” may constitute a ecological/industrial leaning faction of the robot.
  • Every turn, every player chooses their next move on the app interface. After everyone is finished, watch as all of the robots move at once on the board.
    • Players can move 1 tile at a time
    • Players can choose which direction if multiple directions are available
  • Upon conquering a new tile, the robot acquires resources from that tile based on the tile environment (e.g. if it is an eco tile, the robot acquires more eco related items and abilities)
    • If you encounter an enemy (other player) on the same tile, the environment of the tile gives and advantage to the respective items in player inventory.

Thursday // 3.7.2019

The game board has been prototyped, and the size looks good and the variety of tiles is nice. A good variety of map layouts can be achieved with the current tile count and shapes. I think the relationship between the size of the robots and the size of the tiles will be interesting. Most board games have relatively small pieces for the board that they are played on. I think it would be cool/visually engaging if the robots were rather large and became a sort of moving/animated part of the board.
From a first simulated playthrough, I think that this is a good start for what might become a more complex game in further iterations. I think that more thought needs to be put into what the individual attachments do. In order to improve gameplay diversity, I think attachments that add abilities like an additional movement distance (like a head attachment), versus purely combative attachments (like arms) would be a really interesting component that pushes the players to consider their strategy against the other players.
The size of the board is also an interesting component that will have a large impact on the gameplay. A smaller board, like the prototype pictured, will force players to be very resourceful in their first movements. Players will pick up their attachments quickly, and a decisive move will likely decide the rest of the game. A larger board will encourage a little more exploration in the first stage of the game. A player can plan further ahead, and can prioritize their own preferences in the construction of their robot. This also affects a player’s strategy in picking their fights: if another player has certain attachments that would give them the advantage, one might decide to avoid that player until they are fully prepared to fight.
Smaller board:

  • Short games
  • Decisive moves
  • You get what you get... board layout has greater impact on game
Larger board:
  • Long games
  • Cat and mouse
  • Customization of robot
Ideas:
  • Add an additional resource besides attachments
    • Diversify strategy and gameplay
    • Take over the map! → more potential for incorporating the story/world-building
  • Points (resource?) that may decide game → player that reaches point cap wins
    • Reduce cat and mouse gameplay, encourage fighting
  • This game needs an excitement factor… (or maybe it needs strong strategy gameplay at its core?)
    • More tension with strategy
    • Have the pieces fly off when a player is defeated

Week 3

Tuesday // 3.12.2019

The majority of our group project time was spent discussing whether or not the Robions game will be more AR-centric or Physical focused. In the next steps for the game we will need to have a clear direction in which to work. Either side has pros and cons:

Physical:

PROS

  • The tactile and interactive play
  • Imaginative play
  • Board game experience
    • Social group setting
    • Typically no technology involved
  • More simple game mechanics, could be nice
    • Potential to build more robust gameplay mechanics
  • Stronger emphasis on strategy

CONS

  • The forms of the Robions are limited, referential
  • Limited parts, modifiers, win/loss scenarios

AR:

PROS

  • We could have some more complex game mechanics
  • Battles could be fought and decided based on multipliers on the items equipped
  • Battles could be animated
    • More flashy, immersive, discursive
  • The game world could be highly detailed in 3d, and larger than life

CONS

  • The players are in front of screens
    • Removed from the board-game like experience
    • Removed from social experience
    • Distracted from the remaining tactile parts of the game
  • Which physical elements are still important? What do we keep? All digital?
  • The board game element is lost
